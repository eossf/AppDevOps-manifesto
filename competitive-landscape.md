# Competitive Landscape

## Positioning

**Core positioning**: NOT building tools, NOT building frameworks
= **Trusted Advisor** vs. **Vendor Lock-in**

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                   THE ANTI-VENDOR POSITIONING                     │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

   "I teach you to fish, not sell you fish"

   ✓ Not building tools    → They won't be dependent on us

   ✓ Consulting            → Applied to their context

```

## Competitor Types

### 1. Big Consultancies

| Competitor | Examples | Their Strength | Our Advantage |
|------------|----------|----------------|---------------|
| Strategy firms | McKinsey, BCG, Bain | Brand credibility | Cost efficiency |
| Tech consulting | Accenture, Deloitte | Enterprise relationships | Agility |
| Systems integrators | IBM, Capco | Implementation muscle | Niche expertise |

**The trap**: They build solutions on THEIR platforms → client becomes dependent

### 2. AI Tool Vendors (Training Arms)

| Vendor | Examples | Their Strength | Our Advantage |
|--------|----------|----------------|---------------|
| Cloud providers | Microsoft, Google, AWS | Certified programs | Not vendor lock-in |
| AI labs | Anthropic, OpenAI | Platform knowledge | Teach ALL, not just one |
| Dev tools | GitHub, JetBrains | IDE integration | Vendor agnostic |

**The trap**: They train on THEIR tools → incentivized to keep you there

### 3. AI-Focused Startups & Bootcamps

| Type | Examples | Their Strength | Our Advantage |
|------|----------|----------------|---------------|
| AI bootcamps | General bootcamps | Structured curriculum | Enterprise focus |
| Specialized shops | Niche AI firms | Deep expertise | Personal attention |
| Online platforms | Coursera, Udemy | Scale | Live, applied |

**The gap**: Many are consumer/SMB focused, not enterprise-grade

### 4. Individual Consultants

| Type | Examples | Their Strength | Our Advantage |
|------|----------|----------------|---------------|
| Former execs | Ex-CTOs, Ex-VPs | Credibility | Repeatable methodology |
| Freelance AI | Independent advisors | Flexibility | Scale (aspirational) |
| Ex-vendors | Ex-Microsoft, Ex-Google | Platform depth | Broader toolkit |

**The reality**: We ARE this category → differentiation via methodology

## The Three Questions Enterprise Buyers Ask

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│          ENTERPRISE BUYER'S EVALUATION FRAMEWORK                  │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

   Question 1: "Can you solve our problem?"
   → Training content, case studies, credentials
   
   
   Question 2: "Will we become dependent on you?"
   → THIS IS WHERE NOT BUILDING TOOLS WINS
   → If we use their stack (not ours), they can sustain
   → If we teach patterns (not proprietary frameworks), they own it
   
   
   Question 3: "How do we know if it worked?"
   → Measurable outcomes
   → Before/after metrics
   → ROI framework
```

## Competitive Advantages to Emphasize

| Advantage | Description | How to Prove |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| Vendor agnostic | We teach ALL platforms, not just one | Show multi-platform curriculum |
| Knowledge transfer | They leave able to do it themselves | Define "success" metrics upfront |
| PM-centric | Aligns AI to business outcomes | Show PM-focused outcomes |
| Factory model | Structured approach to AI adoption | Provide methodology doc |
| Independence | No lock-in, no ongoing fees | Be explicit about this |

## Threats to Monitor

| Threat | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|--------|------------|------------|
| Big consultancy adds training arm | Medium | Stay niche, stay personal |
| Vendor cert programs become mandatory | Medium | Get certified ourselves, or partner |
| AI tools become "easy" (less need for training) | Low | Move up the value stack (Layer 2-3) |
| Consultants copy our methodology | High | Build brand/methodology reputation |
