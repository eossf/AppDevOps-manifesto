## Executive Summary

**Mission**: Help enterprises adopt AI in development and operations through training and consulting.

**Core Positioning**: Trusted advisor that teaches companies to fish, not sell them fish. We don't build tools or frameworks — we transfer knowledge so companies become independent.

**Target Market**: Companies with 150+ employees, having in-house PM, Dev, and Ops teams.

**Revenue Model**:
| Tier | Type | Price |
|------|------|-------|
| Tier 1: Foundation | Training | $400/person/day |
| Tier 2: Practitioner | Training | $500/person/day |
| Tier 3: Transformation | Consulting | $1,200/day |

**The Flywheel**: Training → Trust → Consulting

**Tier 3: Transformation (on-demand consulting, $1,200/day)**

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Target Company

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Size | 150+ employees |
| Teams | Has in-house PM, Dev, AND Ops |
| Maturity | Some AI adoption attempts (failed or partial) |
| Pain | Productivity goals, cost pressure, competitive threat |
| Trigger | Board pressure, failed AI project, competitor success |

### NOT a Fit

| Attribute | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| No developers | Model requires Dev as actor |
| Pure SaaS, no in-house tech | Can't implement Feature Craft |
| Very early stage | No budget for transformation |

### Buyer Persona

| Role | Title | Pain Points |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Economic Buyer | CTO, VP Engineering | Budget, board pressure, competitive gap |
| User Buyer | PM Director, Dev Lead | Productivity, quality, job satisfaction |
| Champion | AI-forward Dev or PM | Internal credibility, proving value |

## Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|------------|------------|
| Big consultancy adds training arm | Medium | Stay niche, personal |
| Vendor certs become mandatory | Medium | Get certified or partner |
| AI tools become "easy" | Low | Move up to Layer 2-3 |
| Consultants copy methodology | High | Build brand reputation fast |
