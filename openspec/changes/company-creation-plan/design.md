## Context

**Background**: Building a new company that provides AI training and consulting to enterprises (150+ employees). The company operates on a "trusted advisor" model — not building tools or frameworks, but transferring knowledge so clients become independent.

**Current State**:
- AppDevOps Manifesto exists (README.md) in English
- Business model defined in EXPLORE.md
- Competitive analysis in competitive-landscape.md
- Thought leadership strategy in manifesto-company-link.md

**Target Market**:
- Ideal Customer Profile: 150+ employee companies with in-house PM, Dev, and Ops
- Geographic: Initially targeting existing network (likely French companies with global presence)
- Buyer: CTO/VP Engineering (economic), PM Director/Dev Lead (user)

**Stakeholders**:
- Founder (solo operation initially)
- Target clients (PM, Dev, Ops roles)
- AI tool vendors (Microsoft, Google, Anthropic) — not partners, just ecosystem

## Goals / Non-Goals

**Goals:**
- Establish thought leadership via AppDevOps Manifesto publication
- Land first 2-3 training clients within 3-4 months
- Build a repeatable methodology for AI adoption (Feature Craft)
- Create a flywheel: Training → Trust → Consulting

**Non-Goals:**
- Building AI tools, frameworks, or platforms
- Competing with AI vendors (we teach vendor-agnostic approach)
- Targeting companies without developers (Pure SaaS / no-dev companies)
- Global scale in Year 1

## Decisions

### Decision 1: Thought Leadership First

**Choice**: Publish manifesto before selling services
**Rationale**: Establishes credibility, differentiates from generic consultants, attracts inbound leads
**Alternative**: Start selling immediately → Harder to differentiate, no authority

### Decision 2: Tiered Pricing Model

**Choice**: Tier 1-2 as lead generation, Tier 3 as margin driver
**Rationale**:
- Tier 1 ($400/person/day): Low risk, accessible → Gets foot in door
- Tier 2 ($500/person/day): Premium for depth
- Tier 3 ($1,200/day): High trust required, but already know the client from training
**Alternative**: Flat consulting rate → No lead generation mechanism

### Decision 3: Vendor-Agnostic Approach

**Choice**: Teach all platforms, don't certify for specific vendors
**Rationale**: Clients don't want lock-in, independence is a selling point
**Alternative**: Get vendor certified (Microsoft, Google) → Limits credibility with clients using other platforms

### Decision 4: PM-Centric Factory Model as Core Differentiator

**Choice**: Position the "Feature Craft" factory model (PM → AI → Dev) as the methodology
**Rationale**: Unique positioning vs. competitors who focus on Dev-only or tool-only
**Alternative**: Generic AI training → Easily copied, no differentiation

### Decision 5: Personal Network as Initial Sales Channel

**Choice**: Leverage existing contacts for first clients
**Rationale**: Faster to first client, lower customer acquisition cost
**Alternative**: Cold outreach/content marketing → Slower, higher CAC

## Risks / Trade-offs

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Big consultancy adds training arm | Stay niche, personal, PM-centric focus |
| Vendor certs become industry standard | Partner with vendors or get certified if needed |
| AI tools become "easy" (less need for training) | Move up the stack to Layer 2-3 integration |
| Consultants copy methodology | Build brand/methodology reputation quickly |
| Dependent on personal network | Diversify via referrals and case studies |
| Market timing (AI landscape shifting) | Stay current, update curriculum regularly |

## Migration Plan

**Phase 1: Foundation (Month 1)**
1. Publish manifesto on LinkedIn + PDF
2. Set up legal entity
3. Define service offerings and SLAs

**Phase 2: Build Authority (Months 2-3)**
1. LinkedIn content strategy (2-3x/week)
2. Network outreach to warm contacts
3. Attend AI/DevOps meetups

**Phase 3: First Clients (Month 3-4)**
1. Run first Tier 1 workshop
2. Gather testimonials
3. Start lead nurturing for Tier 3

**Phase 4: Scale (Month 6+)**
1. Launch Tier 2, Tier 3 offerings
2. Build case study library
3. Formalize referral program
